How the tax calculation works

Working Holiday Visa holders (subclass 417 and 462) are taxed as non-residents under a special rate set by the ATO. Unlike Australian residents, there is no tax-free threshold — but the first $45,000 is taxed at only 15%, which is lower than the resident rate for that bracket.

The 2024–25 rates applied by this calculator are:

Taxable income Tax rate
$0 – $45,00015%
$45,001 – $135,00032.5%
$135,001 – $190,00037%
Over $190,00045%

Medicare Levy

Most Working Holiday Makers are exempt from the 2% Medicare Levy because Australia has reciprocal health agreements with many countries (including the UK, Ireland, New Zealand, Belgium, Finland, Italy, Malta, the Netherlands, Norway, Slovenia, and Sweden). If your country is not on this list, you may need to pay it. The calculator lets you toggle this on or off.

Superannuation

As a WHV holder working in Australia, your employer is generally required to pay superannuation (currently 11.5% of your ordinary earnings) into a super fund on your behalf. This is on top of your gross pay and is not deducted from your wages. When you leave Australia permanently, you can claim this back as a Departing Australia Superannuation Payment (DASP) — though it is taxed at 65% upon withdrawal for WHV holders.
